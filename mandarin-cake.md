# Mandarin cake

From Gartine's book.

## Cake

Makes 10-12 small cakes or one big one.

* 225 g carrots
* 160 ml sunflower oil
* 2 eggs
* 195 g light brown sugar
* 165 g mandarins
* 70 g almonds
* 250 g self-rising flour (find substitute)
* 1 thick vanilla stick
* 2 tsp cloves

Heat oven to 200°C.
Grease the molds with the extra tablespoon of sunflower oil and line the bottoms with baking paper.

Peel and coarsely grate the carrots.
Beat the oil, eggs, and caster sugar at high speed with a mixer until thick and creamy.
In a food processor, purée the mandarins (with their skins) with the almonds and carrots.
Add the flour, vanilla seeds, and ground cloves and mix briefly.
While still beating, gradually fold the mandarin-carrot mixture into the egg batter.
Beat until well combined, but do not overbeat.

Divide the batter evenly among the molds.
Bake the cakes in the center of the oven for 30 minutes.
Remove the cakes from the oven and let them cool in the liners on a wire rack for 5 minutes.
Remove the cakes from their pans and let them cool completely on a wire rack.

## Frosting

* 140 g room-temperature butter
* 80 g powdered sugar
* 1 tsp orange blossom water
* 100 g cream cheese
* 2 tbsp honey

Make the frosting by thoroughly beating all the ingredients together with a mixer.Refrigerate until the cakes are completely cooled, then frost.
